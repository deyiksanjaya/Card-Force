Pro Force

Created by Heri Sanjaya

Welcome to the operational manual for Pro Force. This application is designed to look like a standard casino game utility used to randomly select a starting card. Beneath the surface, it is a highly customizable, covert forcing tool designed for professional working magicians.

1. The Cover Screen

Upon launching the app, you are greeted with a fake setup screen asking, "What game are we playing today?". This facade disarms the spectator. Tap any game button or click "Skip Setup" to proceed to the main interface.

2. The Peek System

At the bottom center of the main screen, you will see a subtle version number: v2.4.000. This is your visual peek. The last three digits indicate the target card the system has locked onto.

  • First Two Digits (Value): 01 = Ace, 02 to 10 = Spot cards, 11 = Jack, 12 = Queen, 13 = King.
  • Last Digit (Suit): 1 = Spades (♠), 2 = Hearts (♥), 3 = Clubs (♣), 4 = Diamonds (♦).

Example: If the text reads v2.4.112, the forced card will be the Jack of Hearts.

3. The Secret Settings Menu

To access the configuration menu, perform a Triple Tap rapidly on the center of the card. Your settings are saved locally to your device, so you do not need to reconfigure them before every performance.

A. Force Mode

Dictates the sequence of cards forced when the system is activated.

  • Stebbins Stack: Progresses through the classic Si Stebbins order (+3 value, alternating suits).
  • Odd (1,3,5,7,9): Forces only odd numbered cards. Built-in psychology: Low odds (A,3,5) are strictly Red. High odds (7,9) are strictly Black.
  • Even (2,4,6,8,10): Forces only even numbered cards. Low evens (2,4,6) are strictly Red. High evens (8,10) are strictly Black.

B. Shuffle Display

Controls what the spectator sees during the shuffling animation (only applies to Odd/Even modes).

  • All Cards: The entire deck is shown during the blur.
  • Force Only: Only the cards within your chosen mode (e.g., only odd red/black cards) will be flashed during the animation. This provides subliminal conditioning for memory-based forces.

C. JQK Suit (Anim)

If you use the "Force Only" shuffle display, court cards (J,Q,K) are injected into the blur to make the deck feel complete. This setting selects which single suit those court cards will display.

D. Anim Speed

A slider to control the velocity of the shuffling animation. Slide left for a slower, visible flip, or right for a high-speed blur.

E. Ending Mode

  • Normal: The card remains on screen indefinitely after the force.
  • Vanish: Unlocks the ability to manually vanish the card from the screen at the end of your routine (Only available when using the Stebbins mode).

4. Performance Handling & Gestures

The core philosophy of Pro Force is to use standard gestures to separate legitimate interactions from covert triggers.

Normal Shuffle

To prove the app is fair, Tap the card to begin shuffling. Tap again to stop. When initiated with a tap, the system performs a genuine random selection (including court cards). You can still read the peek before stopping.

Activating the Force

When you are ready to activate your chosen force, perform a Swipe (Up or Down) on the card. This movement should be as subtle as possible so it visually mimics a standard tap to the spectator. The device will deliver a subtle haptic vibration to confirm the force is locked, and the shuffle animation will begin immediately.

Force on Stop

Best with: Stebbins Stack or Normal Shuffle Display

After executing the Swipe, ask the spectator to say "stop", then Tap the screen. The card will land precisely on your locked target, allowing you to reveal it via prediction or mind-reading.

Force during Shuffle

Best with: Odd/Even + Force Only Shuffle Display

Instead of stopping the card, ask the spectator to simply stare at the blurring animation and lock onto one card they happen to see passing by. Since you control the exact parameters (e.g., strictly Odd numbers with designated red/black color rules, plus one chosen Court suit), you already know the highly restricted pool of cards they could possibly see. Use classic fishing techniques to seamlessly nail their thought-of card.

Vanish Mode

Before starting the routine, read the visual peek and physically palm the corresponding target card in your hand. Execute a Stebbins force so the target card is displayed on the screen. Give the spectator a moment to register it.

Important Technical Note: The first swipe you perform activates the Stebbins sequence. To execute the vanish, cover the screen with your palming hand while simultaneously executing a subtle secondary Swipe. The second swipe is what triggers the instant vanish of the digital card, leaving the physical card in your hand.

Safety Feature: Once the card vanishes, the screen goes into absolute lockdown. It will ignore all taps and swipes.

5. Routine Idea

  1. Step 1: Arrange your physical deck in a reversed Si Stebbins stack using the "SHoCkeD" suit order (Spades, Hearts, Clubs, Diamonds). Ensure the app is set to Stebbins Stack mode.
  2. Step 2: Read the peek on the app to know the first digital target card. Spread your physical deck, locate that card, and cut it to the top along with the card immediately to its left (which represents the next card in the sequence).
  3. Step 3: Perform a Double Lift to show the top card as your prediction. Have the spectator swipe the app (which triggers the first force) and tap to stop. The digital card perfectly matches your physical prediction.
  4. Step 4: Turn your Double Lift face down. Take the actual top card and place it face-down onto the phone screen, leaving enough room for the spectator to tap.
  5. Step 5: Ask the spectator to tap the screen to shuffle again, and tap once more to stop. Because the app is in Stebbins mode, it automatically forces the next card in the sequence.
  6. Step 6: Have the spectator turn over the physical card resting on the phone. It has visually changed to match the new digital card they just stopped on.

6. Emergency Hard Reset

There is an invisible 80x80 pixel hitbox located in the Top-Right Corner of the screen. Tapping this area will instantly kill the force, reset the card to its face-down state, and ready the app for the next performance. Use this after a Vanish or if you need to abort a routine.
